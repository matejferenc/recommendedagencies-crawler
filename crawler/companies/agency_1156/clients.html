Dixons Carphone

Timeframe: Present

Europe's largest specialist electrical retailing and services company, Dixons Retail plc had a goal - via its retail arm Currys.co.uk, to firmly cement its position as the dominant player in the online retail arena for Cameras within 9 months, and, to take hold of the gap left by competitors Jessops and Comet having exited it. To achieve this within the tight timeframe, Greenlight used a three tiered approach. It undertook extensive keyword research, matched to currys.co.uk product offering. This pinpointed 100 high volume terms that could be leveraged for an optimum outcome. A creative outreach campaign layered around these terms was formulated and rolled out. It featured a photographic challenge publicized on a social media platform. In tandem, on-page technical issues that impacted the retailer's rankings and visibility were identified and fixed. In just 9 months, reach for currys.co.uk in the Camera space more than doubled, sales orders rose 36% pre-campaign period and revenues saw a 41% uplift. Currys garnered a 480 pounds return for every 1 pound spent, putting it firmly in the picture.

L&G Investments

Timeframe: Present

Following the success of their previous award-winning 2013 ISA campaign, Greenlight shifted the emphasis of analysis from search keyword to customer, to build individual forensic profiles, i.e. the online and offline customer journey and channel usage of 326,000 customers across 7 investor segments, over a broad range of demographics. Greenlight's analysis laid the foundations for L&G's Q1 2014 phasing schedule for each marketing channel based on unique segment insights and behaviour. The analysis also uncovered two core but previously overlooked segments which combined, accounted for 45% of the overall online Investment value between Jan-May 2013, equating to ?5million in monetary terms. Until this discovery, the general consensus had been that one of these audience segments did not engage with the brand online. It pinpointed the purchase cycle of each customer segment was longer than previously thought, which in many cases spanned wide across the 4 months of ISA season, as opposed to the assumed final 2.5 months that last click data would suggest. This thereby highlighted the points at which customers could be most influenced, when historically they had been targeted at the point of sale. The analysis also identified areas of wastage from marketing by highlighting which form of messaging and the mix customers responded to best, thereby effecting a cost saving of ?1 million.

Urban Outfitters

Timeframe: Past

Urban Outfitters wanted to increase its visibility, enter more markets in Europe and expand within its target audience. Having a strong brand presence in the UK, the fashion & lifestyle retailer sought to replicate this across the European domains and increase sales. The clothing retailer had launched online in France (.fr) and Germany (.de). However, with no physical stores in France and very few in Germany, its presence was limited in both. This was compounded by its running a European paid search account with all non-UK campaigns grouped into one. Greenlight took a four tiered approach - first to understand Urban Outfitters very niche target audience and what they associate with. Next to split the clothing retailer's paid search account up into the relevant countries. Then to research top couturiers, local trends, and festivals. Finally, build paid search campaigns around these localised for each market such that the new account structure, would pave the way for a smooth roll out into further markets. Greenlight's strategy enabled the clothing retailer to expand from 3 markets to 13 and increase revenues by 105% in just one year. For every 1 pound spent, Urban Outfitters received 12 pounds return.
