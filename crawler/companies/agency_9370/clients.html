Sage Publications

Timeframe: Present

SAGE Publications is a global educational publisher with 1,500 people around the world and a $300m turnover. Previously they had an outdated static HTML site that was time consuming to maintain and update. To coincide with their 50th anniversary, the company wanted a new site that could be updated quickly and easily; a fresh new design that would provide a better experience for their users, and the ability to localise the site for customers in the US, UK, Australia and India. As a result we have developed a fully responsive multi-language and muti-currency website with advanced search and loads of 3rd party integrations. Working with their in-house designers we used Bootstrap for theming to provide a fully responsive site and the new design is clean, modern and easy to navigate. The Sage team now have control over the content structure and can manipulate the menu if needed as well as spinning up marketing sections for specific promotions if needed. The books and journals can be purchased through the website. A custom checkout process was developed as there are different workflows the user can follow depending on what kind of product he is trying to purchase.

Investors in People

Timeframe: Present

Since 1991, Investors in People (IIP) has set the standard for better people management. Their internationally recognised accreditation is held by 14,000 organisations across the world. The Standard defines what it takes to lead, support and manage people well for sustainable results. IIP offers an innovative online assessment platform for the new version of their Standard (version 6). Cameron and Wilding has been providing support by identifying the nature of the bugs experienced and helping stabilise the platform, transitioning to a fully lean and agile organisation and creating new digital products for the future. Our initial challenge was to improve the stability of the platform, train IIP staff to take up the Product Owner role and support them as they transition to an agile mindset. The digital assessment and results analysis platform is based on Drupal 7 using an array of state of the art modules from Drupal's ecosystem. It had originally been specified, designed and developed in a waterfall way. This resulted in the expectation of being able to deliver a lot in a short period of time. IIP was looking to support and stabilise the existing platform and iteratively add new functionality. This is naturally where an agile development process really shines and it became apparent that software quality and organisational agility play really well together. We did not have one solution to fit all problems. Our approach was to spend a couple of weeks on the discovery phase (the equivalent of a sprint 0 in scrum terms) and then report back key findings with senior stakeholders. Our aim was to have complete buy-in with stakeholders as experience has taught us that this is the most important trait for an organisation moving to an agile mindset.

The Economist

Timeframe: Present

1843 Magazine (formerly Intelligent Life) is a bi-monthly, premium magazine published by the Economist Group, which describes its coverage as "the arts, style, food, wine, cars, travel and anything else under the sun, as long as it's interesting". The magazine's digital presence was failing to do the print edition justice. The web, mobile and tablet platforms were not integrated, there was little aesthetic engagement for consumers and lots of duplication of effort for content to be created for three different devices. Beginning March 2016, publisher Economist Group relaunched and rebranded its high-end magazine, Intelligent Life, with a new name, look and feel with the aim of broadening and enhancing the coverage of 1843 beyond Europe to include America and Asia. On this project Cameron & Wilding worked with The Economist on 2 separate phases; 1. Redevelopment of Intelligent Life 2. Rebrand of Intelligent Life to 1843. Phase 1 was composed of large redevelopment tasks, where energy was focused on optimising the structure of the website and creating a more intuitive user experience and content management. The system we built allowed the editors to manage app and web content as one. Using Drupal's flexible permission system, we developed a simple workflow to allow the team to have content published for the app, while having the ability to delay publishing to the website at a later date. Phase 2 composed of rebranding Intelligent Life into 1843 whereby we built a fully responsive tile based layout for the website and adjusted the existing page layouts to align with the brand changes. This increased the relevance of the digital offering to subsequently increase revenue and advertising options for 1843 sponsors. Cameron & Wilding were able to greatly enhance imagery with a lot of built in flexibility, improve security and SEO, create a more engaging user interface, develop a fully responsive website and provide a smooth transition from Intelligent Life to 1843. During the well-structured and productive project a lot was delivered in a very short period of time. The result being a really appealing website with enhanced editing tools that provide a lot of flexibility to the editorial team. Comparing the results to the same period last year, March 7 (official relaunch) to May 19th for both years the results are the following: Page Views: increased by 227% Visits: increased by 295% Uniques: increased by 263% Reduced duplication by 80% This means that there are many more visits to the hub therefore successfully addressing the objective of enhancing the coverage and readership of the publication. Prior to the relaunch processes took 5 times as long to get through as the same work was repeated over 5 platforms. Essentially we cut down work process up to 80% by reducing 5 time duplication processes into one streamlined process. Key benefits of the project: - App and web content are managed as one - Cross-platform development - Greatly enhanced imagery with a lot of built in flexibility - Improved security and SEO - Mobile first, fully responsive website - More engaging user interface - Smooth transition from Intelligent life to 1843

Telegraph Media Group

Timeframe: Present

The Telegraph Media Group (TMG) wanted to improve the stability, content delivery and design of their digital editions of their daily and weekend papers. We were asked to work with them and their contractors to build and test the new app for tablet, mobile and Kindle using Drupal CMS technology, and provide the internal team (editors) with 24/7 support. TMG's award-winning coverage is now available in one easy to navigate mobile and tablet app. Subscribers enjoy news and opinion across business, politics, world affairs, sport and culture, plus the weekend supplements, Stella magazine, interactive picture galleries and all their favourite puzzles. Our challenge was to improve the stability, content delivery and design of TMG's digital editions of their daily and weekend newspapers. TMG wanted a simple Drupal CMS to manage the content and workflow to feed Pugpig, the publishing platform which delivers content to the app built by 3 Pillar Group (3PG). This system will allow TMG sub-editors need be able to manually copy and paste content from the print CMS (DTI) into a Drupal CMS which will feed Pugpig. In-depth knowledge of The Telegraph's way of working, systems, infrastructure and the product was required in order for us to be able to provide the support required on an ongoing basis. Cameron & Wilding team used Drupal 7 technology to develop the CMS system which included code reviews and clean ups. Through extensive knowledge sharing and close collaboration between The Telegraph team and Cameron & Wilding, the app was ready in December 2015. Throughout the project, we built a strong team that developed confidence in the product. The main key benefits of this new system include facilitating the move towards 100% of print items appearing in the digital edition - currently, we are around 93/95%. It will be extremely difficult to move to 100% because of many things that cannot work on mobile such as "classified ads", long lists or content that is just an image and not designed to be responsive.

ATD Travel Services

Timeframe: Past

ATD Travel Services, a successful, rapidly growing online travel company with annual revenues of more than ?50 million needed a new platform for growth. They needed to replace an outdated technological infrastructure that couldn't keep up with their expansion. They required a system to quickly deploy new branded sites with complex ticketing functionality and multiple languages and currencies supported. In response to these needs, we developed a multi-site, multi-currency and multilingual platform using the Drupal CMS. ATD, a large and rapidly growing company in the travel sector, desperately needed to overhaul their existing web infrastructure. The last few years had seen this company go from a small, niche player, to an established company with annual revenues of more than ?50 million. Their current site was no longer fit for purpose, creaking under the strain of growth. Adding new products, maintaining existing ones, extracting sales data and integrating external APIs took inordinate amounts of time and effort. ne of the key factors of this client's success was branding and white-labelling. They have developed several strong brands and have several white-labelled sites, creating multiple revenue streams from several different countries. At a high level, the requirements for the new platform were: - A multi-site platform - Easy to create new sites for future brands - Content shared across the brands, but with brand-specific fields - Separate user registration for each brand - Different currencies for each brand - Different language for each brand. To determine the best solution, we did a full review of the available options, including Drupal multisite and XML-RPC between separate sites. We decided on using Domain Access module to provide the multisite functionality. Domain Access allows multiple sites to be driven from one database and codebase. Content can be shared between domains, the code is easily maintainable and only a single database is required. Ubercart was used to provide the ecommerce functionality. Although Ubercart provides a full ecommerce solution, a significant amount of custom code was required to provide the exact functionality. This included: - Different prices per domain - Multiple cost currencies - Multiple price currencies - Different credit card options and prices per domain. A host of other features were also developed through a combination of existing modules and custom code. These include: an affiliate system, charting and reporting functionality and an enhanced backend for booking. The client now has a powerful and scalable platform for growth. New brands, currencies and languages can easily be added allowing the client to execute their strategy of launching in new territories, building new brands and providing white-labelled solutions to third parties. - An integrated and comprehensive financial and sales reporting system - A powerful, scalable web platform that supports multiple brands, currencies and languages - Ability to provide white label solutions to third parties
